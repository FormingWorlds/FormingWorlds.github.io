Simulate the coupled interior-atmosphere evolution of rocky planets over deep time.

Named after the Greek god of sea change, PROTEUS is flexible and adaptable; it can foretell the future, but answers only to those who ask the right questions.

A modular framework for planetary science

PROTEUS couples independently developed physics modules into a single evolutionary simulation. Each module can be swapped, configured, or extended.

Atmosphere

Radiative-convective and prescribed atmosphere models (AGNI, JANUS) with chemical kinetics (VULCAN) and hydrodynamic escape (ZEPHYRUS).

Interior

Magma ocean dynamics (Aragog, SPIDER), interior structure (Zalmoxis), and volatile outgassing (CALLIOPE, Atmodeller).

Stellar & tidal

Stellar luminosity evolution (MORS) and tidal heating from solid-phase (LovePy) and multi-phase (Obliqua) models.

From magma oceans to habitable worlds

PROTEUS resolves the full evolutionary arc: from a molten post-impact state through volatile outgassing, atmospheric build-up, and ocean condensation.

  • Sub-Neptunes, super-Earths, and terrestrial planets
  • Coupled interior-atmosphere-escape evolution
  • Synthetic observables for JWST and future missions
  • Open source under the Apache 2.0 licence
